What is Kanban ?

Kanban is a visual workflow management technique that is used in software development, project management, and manufacturing, among other disciplines, to increase productivity, efficiency, and task monitoring. The name comes from the Japanese word "kanban," which's meaning is "visual card" or "signboard."

Key Concepts of Kanban

  1. Visualize Workflow:
    • Kanban uses a board (physical or digital) with columns representing stages in a process.
    • Cards or tasks move across the columns, representing their progress.
  2. Limit Work in Progress (WIP):
    • Restrict the number of tasks that can be in a particular stage at the same time.
    • Helps prevent bottlenecks and ensures tasks are completed efficiently.
  3. Focus on Flow:
    • Emphasizes a smooth and continuous flow of work.
    • Ensures tasks move steadily from start to finish.
  4. Make Process Policies Explicit:
    • Clearly define rules for how tasks are prioritized and moved between stages.
  5. Continuous Improvement:
    • Teams regularly review workflows to identify inefficiencies and make adjustments.
